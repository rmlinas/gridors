<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mt-12 mb-6">Common Mistakes Beginners Make in Homesteading</h2>
<p class="text-lg text-gray-700">Many beginners start with unrealistic expectations. Begin with small, manageable projects. Another common mistake is insufficient planning. Thoroughly research your chosen projects and ensure you have the necessary resources and knowledge. Don't be afraid to ask for help from experienced homesteaders or join a local community.</p>

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mt-12 mb-6">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<p class="font-semibold text-gray-800 mb-2"><strong>Q: How much land do I need to start homesteading?</strong></p>
<p class="text-gray-700">A: You don't need a large plot of land to start homesteading. Even a small balcony or backyard can be used to grow food or raise chickens. The amount of land you need will depend on your goals and the scale of your projects.</p>
<p class="font-semibold text-gray-800 mb-2"><strong>Q: How much money do I need to start homesteading?</strong></p>
<p class="text-gray-700">A: Homesteading can be started on a budget. Focus on small, affordable projects initially. Prioritize free or low-cost resources like using seeds from last year’s harvest and repurposing materials. Gradually scale up as your skills and resources grow.</p>
<p class="font-semibold text-gray-800 mb-2"><strong>Q: What are some essential skills for homesteading?</strong></p>
<p class="text-gray-700">A: Essential skills include basic gardening, food preservation, and basic home repairs. The specific skills you need will depend on your goals and the type of homesteading you pursue.</p>
